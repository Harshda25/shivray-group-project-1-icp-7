Historical Forts

"Shivaji Maharaj was attributed to have had a control of almost 360 forts in his reign"


Shivneri Fort

Shivneri Fort, situated near Junnar in the Pune district of Maharashtra, holds profound historical significance as the birthplace of Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj in 1630. Perched atop a hill, the fort served as a natural fortress, shaping Shivaji's early life and fostering his deep-rooted connection to Marathi culture and language. Shivneri, with its strategic location and historical resonance, stands as a testament to the beginnings of Shivaji's legacy and his eventual role in shaping the Maratha Empire.


Raigad Fort

Located in the Raigad district, Raigad Fort is an iconic symbol of Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj's visionary leadership. After his coronation in 1674, Raigad became the capital of the Maratha Empire. The fort, strategically designed with architectural , reflects the military and administrative prowess of Shivaji Maharaj reign. Raigad witnessed pivotal moments in Maratha history, making it a pilgrimage site for those seeking to understand the strategic brilliance that defined Ch. Shivaji Maharaj rule.

Rajgad Fort

Rajgad Fort,originally known as Murumbdev,is a monumental stronghold situated near Pune in the Sahyadri mountain range.It played a pivotal role in Shivaji's early military campaigns and served as a key bastion for securing his territories.The fortification of Rajgad showcases Shivaji foresight in military strategy, emphasizing its significance of the Maratha kingdom. Rajgad's historical importance and strategic location make it an integral part of the narrative of Shivaji's reign.

Panhala Fort

Perched in the Sahyadri mountains near Kolhapur, Panhala Fort is a testament to Shivaji's commitment to securing key locations in his kingdom. The fort played a crucial role in Shivaji's defensive strategies, serving as a stronghold in the western region. Its architectural significance, coupled with Shivaji's extension and fortification efforts, reflects the importance placed on Panhala in maintaining control over the Deccan plateau.

Shindhudurg Fort

Sindhudurg Fort, situated on an island off the Malvan coast, is a manifestation of Shivaji Maharaj maritime vision. Built under his personal supervision. The architectural brilliance of Sindhudurg, with its concealed entrance and robust bastions, underscores Shivaji Maharaj commitment to maritime security. The fort stands as a testament to Ch. Shivaji Maharaj multifaceted approach in safeguarding his kingdom from both land and sea.

Pratapgad Fort

Pratapgad is mountain fort located in satara district in the Western indian state of Maharashtra, Pratapgad Fort holds historical significance for being the site of the Battle of Pratapgad in 1659. This pivotal battle saw Shivaji Maharaj exhibiting exceptional bravery, defeating the formidable Afzal Khan. The fort's strategic location and its role in this historic event make it a symbol of Shivaji's military acumen and resilience against external threats.

Sinhgad Fort

He instead of having his son’s marriage gave the first preference to go fulfill the task to capture the fort. He was also known as Lion “Sinha In Marathi” of Maratha emperor and to give him the treaty Shivaji Maharaj renamed this fort as Sinhagad. The famous line said by Shivaji Maharaj as ”गड आला पण सिंह गेला” is related to this fort which means we have captured the fort but we have lost our lion warrior.

Vijaydurg Fort

Vijaydurg Fort,situated on the western coast of Maharashtra, India, is a historic sea fort that played a crucial role in maritime activities during the Maratha Empire. Originally known as "Gheria," it was later renamed Vijaydurg, meaning"Fort of Victory."The fort was constructed by Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj and is renowned for its strategic location on the Arabian Sea. Shivaji Maharaj captured Vijaydurg in 1653, and it remained a significant naval base.The fort's architecture includes strong walls,bastions,and a well-designed entrance.

Torna Fort

Torna Fort, also known as Prachandagad, is a historic hill fort nestled in the Sahyadri mountain range,approximately 50 kilometers southwest of Pune,Maharashtra.Dating back to the 13th century,it holds the distinction of being the first fort captured by the legendary Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj in 1646,showcasing his early military prowess.,Its elevation of 1,403 meters makes it the highest fort in Pune district.Torna is surrounded by lush greenery, providing captivating views of the Sahyadri mountains.
